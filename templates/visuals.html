DATA VISUALIZATIONS

Word Cloud


Our initial dataset consisted of 10,607,577 words across all of the news articles combined. After pre-processing our data from removing stop words and lemmatization, our final dataset consisted of 214,805 unique words. The following word cloud was a visual depiction of all of the unique words trained into our model, varying in size depending on the frequency they showed up across all trained news articles.

Stacked Bar


We were interested in looking at the top words that showed up in our dataset, as well as what the distribution of the words being found in fake and real news looked like. The top words were mainly related to U.S. presidents, most notably Donald Trump. Interestingly, news articles related to Obama and Clinton had a higher distribution of fake news compared to Donald Trump.

Bar Chart


Our dataset could be classified into three main topics - Politics, World News, and U.S. News. A majority of our training data consisted of news topics related to politics. Understanding this, the confidence of the model returning an accurate prediction on non-political news is not great.

Confusion Matrix


Logistic Regression was used to train our machine learning model. The training data yielded in an accuracy score of 99.5%. The testing data yielded in an accuracy score of 99.1%.
